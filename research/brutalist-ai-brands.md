# 🏗️ Brutalist & Minimalist AI Brand Design

**Research for "Get a Robert" — May 29, 2026**

## The Challenge
Tyler's product pillars: less is more, respect the intellect, it's a person not a product. The design needs to feel minimal but WARM — brutalist without the cold. An AI companion that looks like a person texting you, not a SaaS dashboard.

## Key Findings

### The Warm/Minimal Tension
- **Brutalist web design** = raw, unpolished, utilitarian. Often reads as cold or confrontational.
- **Minimalist design** = clean, spacious, calm. Can feel sterile if overdone.
- **The sweet spot**: minimalism that uses warmth signals — rounded type, soft colors, human language, generous whitespace. Think: a quiet room, not an empty warehouse.

### Brands Doing This Well

| Brand | Style | Why It Works | Risk |
|-------|-------|-------------|------|
| **Linear** | Ultra-clean, dark, minimal | Feels like a tool built by people who care. No fluff. | Can feel exclusive/intimidating |
| **Vercel** | Geometric, black/white, sharp | Design-as-performance. Every pixel intentional. | Cold if you're not a developer |
| **Stripe** | Colorful minimal, gradients | Warmest of the "dev tools" — approachable sophistication | Enterprise vibe creeping in |
| **Notion** | Clean, illustration-heavy | Friendly minimalism. The illustrations add warmth. | Getting bloated |
| **Things (Cultured Code)** | Warm white, human language | Reads like a conversation. "Hello." not "Dashboard" | Product-specific, not AI |
| **Arc Browser** | Bold, colorful, opinionated | "Meet your new internet" — personified a tool | Heavy marketing, not minimalist |
| **Mymind** | Ultra-minimal, private, quiet | No social, no sharing, no noise. "Peace of mind." | Almost invisible — hard to discover |
| **Day One** | Warm, journal-like, personal | Feels like YOUR space. Gentle. | Looks like a journal, not an AI |

### What Applies to AI Companions
- **Language > layout.** The first thing you see shouldn't be a UI — it should be a message. Like opening your texts.
- **One color that feels human.** Not brand colors. A single warm accent. Think amber, rose, soft teal.
- **The cursor/typing indicator is part of the design.** The feeling of someone on the other end.
- **No "onboarding" screens.** The conversation IS the onboarding (matches Tyler's script).

### Brutalist Lessons
- **Honesty.** Brutalism works when it says "what you see is what you get." For an AI companion: no fake features, no "I'm your new best friend" — just presence.
- **Typography as personality.** A single typeface choice says more than a logo.
- **Negative space invites filling.** An empty chat with one message feels more intimate than a full dashboard.
