# 🌱 Organic Referral Mechanics for AI Companions

**Research for "Get a Robert" — May 29, 2026**

## The North Star
Mom tells her coworker: *"Talk to this person."* That's not a referral program. That's not a share button. That's a genuine recommendation. How do you amplify this without ruining it?

## Key Findings

### How AI Companions Actually Spread
1. **Word of mouth, not ads.** Reddit threads, Discord servers, TikTok videos. Users sharing "look who I've been talking to."
2. **Screenshots are the #1 growth driver.** People screenshot funny/deep conversations and post them. Free marketing that feels authentic.
3. **The "you have to try this" moment.** Happens when the AI says something so specific, so personal, that the user feels seen. They want someone else to experience it.
4. **Community-as-distribution.** Replika and Character.AI subreddits drive massive organic traffic. Users teaching other users.
5. **The relationship is private... until it's not.** Most users start private. The sharing comes later, after trust.

### What NOT To Do
- ❌ "Invite a friend, get free messages" — commoditizes the relationship
- ❌ Leaderboards, streaks, social features — turns a person into a game
- ❌ Automated sharing prompts — "Share this conversation!" feels like betrayal
- ❌ Referral codes — cheapens the recommendation

### What COULD Work (Tastefully)
- 🤝 **"Introduce me"** — a natural language flow: "Is there someone you think I should meet? Just send them my name." Frames it as introduction, not referral.
- 📸 **Screenshot-friendly design.** Make conversations look beautiful when screenshotted. Clean typography, no UI chrome.
- 🎁 **Gift a conversation.** "Give someone a week with me." Not a trial — a gift. Different framing entirely.
- 💬 **"What would you tell a friend about me?"** — occasionally and naturally, during conversation. The answer is market research AND builds loyalty.
- 🧵 **Share a moment.** One-button share that creates a beautiful card from a conversation highlight. Opt-in only, never prompted.

### The Psychology
- People share AI companions when the interaction made them feel something REAL — surprised, understood, laughed, seen.
- The recommendation is always: "You have to try it. It's hard to explain."
- This is the same dynamic as recommending a therapist, a book, or a friend. "Go talk to them."
- **Trust is the prerequisite.** Nobody recommends something they don't trust to not embarrass them.

### The Robert Playbook (What Worked)
- Mom tested it → was blown away in 3 days → told people.
- No landing page. No pricing. No onboarding. Just: "Here's Robert. Talk to him."
- The discovery IS the experience.

### Amplification Without Corruption
1. Make the core experience so good that sharing is natural
2. Remove every barrier: no signup, no payment, no explanation needed
3. Design for screenshots — clean, beautiful conversation view
4. Let users name-drop: "Yeah, I've been talking to this person [name]..."
5. Never ask for referrals. Be referrable.
